# Research Rules

## Coverage Standard

Default to a useful public-evidence check, not full diligence. A good verification pass tests the core claims directly and stops once the verdict is defensible.

## Confidence Levels

Use these levels in the final report:

- `high`: multiple strong and current public sources clearly support the claim status
- `medium`: useful evidence exists, but coverage, freshness, or source quality is limited
- `low`: evidence is thin, conflicted, stale, or weakly attributable

## Claim Ledger Discipline

Do not verify the idea as a single blob. Break it into discrete claims first.

Good claim ledger behavior:

- 3-7 claims
- each claim is testable
- each claim has evidence mapped to it
- each claim ends in one verdict bucket

Bad claim ledger behavior:

- one giant claim like `the business will work`
- claims with no evidence mapping
- verdicts based on overall vibe

## Verdict Buckets

Use only these verdicts:

- `supported`
- `contradicted`
- `unclear`

`supported` means the public evidence directly leans in favor of the claim.

`contradicted` means the public evidence directly weakens or opposes the claim.

`unclear` means the evidence is not enough, conflicts, or depends on non-public information.

## Freshness Discipline

If the claim depends on current platform behavior, policy, pricing, or market activity, prioritize current sources and note dates explicitly.

Do not treat stale sources as strong proof for fast-moving categories.

## Anti-Handwaving Rules

Never do the following:

- treat competitor existence as proof of demand
- treat lack of visible competitors as proof of opportunity
- treat social engagement as proof of profitable demand
- treat one anecdote as category validation
- treat missing evidence as silent support

These are the most common failure modes in idea evaluation.

## Commercial Judgment Layer

After the claim ledger is scored, ask:

- What is the single strongest support for the idea?
- What is the single strongest weakening fact?
- Is the key uncertainty about demand, channel, competition, trust, or feasibility?
- What should be validated next with primary research or internal data?

Good commercial judgments are:

- claim-led
- evidence-backed
- specific about what is supported versus unresolved

Examples:

- `The demand premise looks plausible, but the TikTok Shop acquisition premise is under-supported for this price point.`
- `The idea is not obviously invalid, but the differentiation claim is weak because incumbents already occupy the premium-trust position.`
- `Public evidence supports category activity, but not enough to validate this exact target customer or pricing posture.`

## Honest Gaps

Always say when you could not verify:

- conversion economics
- margins
- CAC
- retention
- supplier reality
- platform approval certainty

These often require private or primary data.
