# Source Adapters

Use this file when deciding how to gather and describe evidence from different public sources.

**For every channel below, you must navigate to and read the actual page.** Load the source page directly. WebSearch snippets are not evidence — they are truncated and may misrepresent the content. If you cannot load the actual page, mark the gap rather than using a snippet as a substitute.

## Official Brand Or Product Pages

- Treat these as primary evidence for positioning, claims, assortment, and offer design.
- Record what the brand says clearly and avoid paraphrasing away important wording differences.
- Do not treat brand claims as proof by themselves.

## Collection Or Category Pages

- Use these for assortment depth, merchandising structure, and price ladder context.
- Watch for stale sale prices or unavailable variants.
- If category pages are cluttered, use them for structure rather than detailed claim analysis.

## Amazon And Marketplaces

- Use for price anchors, review volume, visible product framing, and competitive packaging.
- Separate marketplace SEO copy from differentiated positioning.
- Treat seller, fulfillment, and counterfeit issues separately from product comparison unless they clearly affect conversion.

## Retailer Product Pages

- Use when the retailer surface is cleaner or more credible than the DTC page.
- Useful for third-party trust context and normalized PDP comparison.
- Keep track of whether the retailer is adding its own merchandising language.

## Reddit And Community Threads

- Use for language richness, buyer objections, and category perception.
- Treat as noisier evidence than official pages and normalized review systems.
- Prefer firsthand use or comparison threads over vague recommendation lists.

## TikTok, YouTube, And Public Social Surfaces

- Use when visible messaging, product demos, creator reactions, or repeated objections are clear.
- Treat viral anecdotes carefully.
- Use social content as supporting evidence, not the sole basis for strategic claims.

## Review Surfaces

- Use for trust proof and visible objection patterns.
- A light review read can support competitor comparison.
- If complaint patterns become the center of the analysis, move into a dedicated `find-bad-review` pass.

## Unknown Or Weak Sources

- Downgrade evidence quality when the source is scraped, snippet-only, or hard to attribute.
- If product matching is weak, keep the note but do not let it dominate the conclusions.
