# Comparison Lenses

Use this file to decide what dimensions belong in the analysis. Choose only the lenses that help answer the user's question.

## Positioning

Use when the question is about who each competitor is "for" and what promise they lead with.

Look for:

- premium vs value posture
- performance vs style emphasis
- functional vs emotional framing
- professional, enthusiast, or everyday-user tone

## Pricing

Use when price is part of the comparison question or likely to affect conversion.

Look for:

- price point
- price band
- anchor pricing
- discounting or bundle logic
- whether price is justified by visible proof or claims

## Offer Structure

Use when comparing how the product is packaged and sold.

Look for:

- single-SKU vs bundle
- starter kit vs refill or accessory logic
- subscription or repeat-purchase mechanics
- warranty, returns, or guarantees

## Assortment Depth

Use when brand strength may come from breadth, specialization, or merchandising depth.

Look for:

- number of variants
- size or color depth
- adjacent products
- category specialization vs generalist catalog

## Claims And Benefits

Use when the comparison depends on what each competitor says the product does.

Look for:

- functional claims
- proof-backed claims
- differentiated materials or engineering
- emotional or lifestyle benefits

## Trust And Proof

Use when conversion confidence is part of the question.

Look for:

- review count and average rating
- UGC, before/after, or testimonials
- expert endorsements
- visible guarantees
- press, certifications, or retailer credibility

## Review Profile

Use when you need a lightweight reputation view rather than a full bad-review report.

Look for:

- dominant positive themes
- dominant negative themes
- whether praise and complaints support or undercut the brand's claims

If review weaknesses are the main question, consider pairing or following up with `find-bad-review`.

## Conversion Clarity

Use when evaluating product pages, PDPs, landing pages, or DTC offers.

Look for:

- message clarity above the fold
- obviousness of the core offer
- speed of objection handling
- CTA clarity
- whether trust proof appears early enough

## Channel Presence

Use when the question depends on where competitors show up publicly.

Look for:

- DTC only vs marketplace-heavy mix
- retailer distribution
- social presence with visible product messaging
- whether the brand seems native to a specific channel

## What To Avoid

Do not include a lens just because it is available. Too many lenses produce flat, unfocused comparisons.

Weak lens selection:

- everything visible on the page
- equal attention to minor details and commercial drivers

Strong lens selection:

- 3-6 lenses tied directly to the user's decision
